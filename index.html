Cognac

Did You Know That:

Is a variety of brandy named after the commune of Cognac, France. It is produced in the surrounding wine-growing region in the departments of Charente and Charente-Maritime.

The first known reference to “Cognac” meaning brandy appeared in a sales contract in 1617. In 1624, a distillery was established at Tonnay-Charente on the Charente River, about 40 miles north west of Cognac.
